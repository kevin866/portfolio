Projects

Waypoint Profiler Capstone Design Project

For my senior design project, I collaborated with another mechanical engineering student and two computer engineering students to develop an underwater robot. Through stakeholder interviews and trade-off analyses, we prioritized reliability and functionality in the design. We applied DFM principles and FEA simulations to ensure the robot's ease of manufacture and structural reliability. Adhering to strict safety protocols, we safely integrated high-capacity electronics, including thrusters and Lithium-ion batteries. We also implemented an assembly and pre-deployment review process to ensure safe and successful operations. Throughout the project, we maintained effective communication and co-authored a comprehensive 90+ page report. Our efforts earned us the Best in Interdisciplinary Session award at the Senior Design Presentations.

Robot Manipulator Program for Tool Sorting

I designed a tool-sorting system to improve workbench organization for engineers and technicians in a simulated environment. The project involved programming a robotic arm to sort tools into designated bins. I also developed a user-friendly interface and implemented a voice control system for intuitive, hands-free operation. This solution enhanced workspace efficiency and minimized manual effort through seamless user interaction and automated sorting.

Angular position control with fan motor

In this mechatronics project, I implemented PID control to regulate the angular position of the Lazy Susan by adjusting the motor output effort of the fan motor. I used a variable resistor to measure the system's angle.

Marble Maze Arcade Machine

In this mechatronics project, I 3D printed the main structure of the machine, including the ball support joint and the maze. The user can use the joystick to control the orientation of the maze in order to roll the marble ball from the starting point to the endpoint. We implemented a timing function and monitor to enhance the user experience.
